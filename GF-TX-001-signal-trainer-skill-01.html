SKILL 01 // EVALUATIVE
Clarify · Reveal · Reframe

Does this clarify, reveal, or reframe?
Or is it just adding to the pile?

This is the first and most fundamental question in the Gravificer system. Before anything ships, it has to pass this test. But passing the test requires understanding what each of these three moves actually is — because they are distinct operations, not synonyms.

Clarify
Takes something complex and compresses it to its essential structure. The reader understands something they couldn't hold before. The complexity doesn't disappear — it becomes navigable.
Reveal
Makes visible something that was present but unnamed. A pattern, a tension, a relationship that existed in the material but had no form until this piece gave it one.
Reframe
Changes the frame around the same facts. The reader now sees the same material as a different kind of problem — or a different kind of opportunity. The facts haven't changed. What they mean has.

Work that does none of these things is noise — regardless of its quality, accuracy, or polish. In this trainer, you'll be shown pieces of work and asked to identify which move they're making — or whether they're making any move at all. Claude will diagnose your read and sharpen your perception over a series of scenarios.
